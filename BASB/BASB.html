The C.O.D.E. Method (Cooler Organized Dude Energy)

C - Capture (Keep the cool stuff)

Collect information around you that seems interesting into your note-taking app, like collecting Krusty the Clown trading cards.

What to capture:

  • Ideas for awesome pranks
  • Content that answers your burning questions
  • Information that makes you go "¬°Ay, caramba!"

What to avoid keeping:

  • Homework assignments
  • Chalkboard detention writings
  • Principal Skinner's boring announcements
Listen to your gut‚Äîif something inside you says "cowabunga," trust it and capture it.

O - Organize (File it like Lisa would)

Organize using the PARA Method (even though organization is totally for nerds):

  • Pranks - Short-term mischief in progress
  • Awesome stuff - Ongoing cool things you're into
  • Radical knowledge - Topics that might be useful for future schemes
  • Ancient history - Old stuff you don't need right now

Plus a messy desk (inbox) for tossing new stuff until you deal with it.

In PARA, Pranks and Awesome stuff are what you're doing now, while Radical knowledge and Ancient history are just there when you need them.

D - Distill (Find the secret sauce)

Find the essence of your notes through progressive summarization (like cheating on a test but legal):

  1. Bold the parts that would impress Lisa
  2. Highlight the words that would make Homer say "Woo-hoo!"
  3. Summarize for Bart-sized attention spans
  4. Write the "eat my shorts" version

If you have limited time, read only the bold parts. If detention is extra long, read everything.

Just make your note slightly less lame every time you touch it. Don't try to do it all during one detention session.

E - Express (Show off your work)

Use your knowledge to create cool stuff and share it with Springfield. All the feedback we receive is a chance to make our next prank even better.

How to express Bart-style:

  1. Break big projects into skateboard-sized chunks
  2. Keep your treehouse organized with the PARA method
  3. Use progressive summarization (like Cliff's Notes but better)
  4. Embrace imperfection ‚Äì "Just do it, man!"
An idea wants to be shared. And in the sharing, it becomes more complex, more interesting and more likely to freak out more adults.

Maintenance Habits

Like your skateboard, your Second Brain also needs maintenance to work properly and efficiently:

  • Use prank checklists to avoid getting caught
  • Do weekly and monthly reviews while sitting in detention
  • Regularly ask yourself "What Would Bart Do?" in tricky situations
  • Organize your secret hideouts containing relevant notes
  • Search for related pranks across folders for maximum impact
Habits reduce brain work and free up mental space, so you can focus on what really matters... skateboarding and avoiding homework! It's only by making the boring stuff easier that you can create the mental space needed for planning the perfect prank.
